Copyright and other constraints affecting websites

Copyright is the right of someone who creates an original image. As soon as an image is created it has copyright and the person who created it owns the rights to that image. Only the owner has the right to reproduce the work, display the work in public and copy the work or sell copies of the work to other people. The same applies to programs, you must create your own.


Access issues

Access issues could include people with disabilities, and therefore make it difficult for them to navigate your website. A basic example is someone who is blind, to make a website accessible to them, you could make effects for navigation. You could make auto answers to select from to enable a mouse only website, to navigate easier.


Appropriate filetypes

For this website, all I used was one CSS file, for the layout and design of the webste. I used 5 HTML pages, one for each page of the website, and jpg files for the images, although other image files are also usable. Pyhton or JS files could also be used to make a more dynamic website.
